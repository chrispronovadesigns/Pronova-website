## Why most app projects fail

It's not usually a technology problem. It's a communication problem.

Most developers jump straight to building. They take a spec document, disappear into a code cave for three months, and come back with something that technically matches the requirements but doesn't actually work the way your business operates. By that point, you've already paid 50-70% of the budget, and you're stuck choosing between scrapping it or paying more to fix it.

**The pattern looks like this:**

Vague requirements lead to wrong assumptions. Wrong assumptions lead to features nobody asked for and missing ones everybody needed. Misalignment gets discovered late — after the money's spent. And the whole thing spirals.

We've watched this happen to enough businesses to know exactly where it breaks down. The fix isn't better code — it's a better process that keeps you in the loop at every stage so nothing gets built that doesn't match what you actually need.

## How we build apps differently

Our process is designed around one principle: **you should never be surprised by what you're paying for.**

### Phase 1 — Discovery and planning (weeks one and two)

Before we write a single line of code, we sit down — in person if possible — and map out your entire workflow. Not just what the app should do, but *why*. What's the manual process it replaces? Who uses it? What happens when something goes wrong? Where does data need to flow?

**Deliverables:** Detailed requirements document, system architecture plan, user flow diagrams, wireframes for every screen. You sign off on all of this before development begins.

### Phase 2 — Design (weeks two and three)

We design the full user interface — every screen, every interaction, every edge case. You see exactly what your app will look like and how it will work before it's built. This is where we catch 90% of the "wait, that's not what I meant" moments.

**Deliverables:** Interactive prototype you can click through on your phone. Full UI design kit. Feedback rounds until you're genuinely happy — not just "fine with it."

### Phase 3 — Development (weeks three through ten)

We build the front-end and back-end simultaneously. You get a staging environment you can log into at any time to see progress. Bi-weekly demos where we walk you through what's been built. No black box, no guessing.

**Technology stack:** We use React Native for cross-platform mobile development (your app works on both iOS and Android from a single codebase — saving you roughly 40% vs. building each separately). Node.js on the server side. Cloud infrastructure that scales with your user base.

**Deliverables:** Working builds at each milestone. Access to a staging environment. Bi-weekly progress demos.

### Phase 4 — Testing and optimization (weeks ten through thirteen)

Real users test the app. We find and fix issues. We optimize performance so the app loads fast, handles edge cases, and doesn't crash when 500 people use it at once.

**Deliverables:** QA testing report, performance benchmarks, user acceptance testing with your team.

### Phase 5 — Launch and deployment (weeks thirteen through sixteen)

We deploy the app to the iOS App Store, Google Play Store, and/or the web. We handle all the submission requirements, review processes, and configuration. Your app goes live, and we make sure it stays live.

**Deliverables:** Published apps on all target platforms. Server deployment. Admin dashboard/CMS if applicable. Training session for your team.

People always want to know what technology we use and why. Here's the straightforward version:

**React Native** for mobile development. Your app runs natively on both iOS and Android from one codebase. This isn't a compromise — apps like Instagram, Walmart, and Discord all use React Native. You get native performance without paying for two completely separate development teams.

We build with technologies that have huge communities, proven track records, and long futures. Nothing experimental that might leave you stranded in two years.

## What a project actually looks like

### Case study: Hexatronic — Field technician management app

**The problem:** Hexatronic needed to track field technicians across multiple job sites in real time. Their existing process was a patchwork of phone calls, spreadsheets, and manual check-ins. Dispatchers couldn't see who was where, technicians couldn't update job status on the fly, and the whole operation was bleeding efficiency.

**What we built:** A multi-platform mobile and web application with real-time geo-location tracking, two-factor authentication, an admin dashboard for dispatchers, and a content management system for updating job details and assignments.

**How it went:** Discovery and design took two weeks. Front-end development for iOS, Android, and web took five weeks. Back-end and integration took three weeks. Testing and optimization took three weeks. Full deployment across all platforms in week 16.

**The result:** A system that replaced manual coordination with real-time visibility. Dispatchers see every technician's location and job status live. Technicians update progress from their phones. The entire operation runs tighter, faster, and with fewer missed handoffs.
