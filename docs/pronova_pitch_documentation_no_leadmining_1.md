## UI/UX design

**Design that works before it looks good**

### One-line pitch

We design digital experiences that feel natural to your users — and drive the specific actions your business needs.

### Elevator pitch

Beautiful design that nobody can figure out how to use is just expensive art. And ugly design that works great — well, nobody sticks around long enough to find out. ### Full pitch

**The real cost of bad design**

Most businesses think of design as the visual layer — colors, fonts, layouts. That's a piece of it. But the design decisions that actually affect your revenue are invisible to most people: where buttons are placed, how forms are structured, what information appears first, how navigation flows from one screen to the next.

Bad design doesn't just look unprofessional — it actively costs you money. **88% of users** won't return to a site after a bad experience. **70% of online businesses** fail because of poor usability. Every confusing menu, every buried call to action, every extra click between your user and their goal is revenue walking out the door.

**Our design process puts function first**

We don't start with mood boards or color palettes. We start with questions: Who's using this? What do they need to accomplish? What's getting in their way right now? What action do you need them to take?

From there, we build:

**User research and personas** — real data about your users, not assumptions. Who they are, what they want, what frustrates them.

**Information architecture** — the structural blueprint that determines how content is organized and how users navigate through it.

**Wireframes** — low-fidelity layouts that let us test structure and flow before anyone worries about visual polish.

**Visual design** — the interface comes to life with your brand's identity, purposeful color systems, typography that aids readability, and visual hierarchy that guides the eye.

**Interactive prototyping** — a clickable version of the product that lets you test the experience before development begins. This is where we catch problems that cost 10x more to fix in code.

**Usability testing** — real users interact with the prototype while we watch, measure, and refine.

Whether we're designing a standalone interface or the front end of an app or website we're building, the process is the same: understand the problem, design the solution, test it, refine it, build it.
