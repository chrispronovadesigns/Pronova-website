SECTION 03
Logo usage


Logo configurations
The Pronova logo consists of two elements: the geometric hexagonal mark and the PRONOVA wordmark. These can be used in three configurations:



Color variations
The logo appears in four color variations depending on background:
Full color on dark: Primary version. Hexagonal mark in brand colors on obsidian or charcoal backgrounds.
Full color on light: For light backgrounds. Mark retains brand colors, wordmark in obsidian.
White: All-white version for use over images, gradients, or colored backgrounds.
Obsidian: All-dark version for light print materials and formal documents.

Clear space and minimum size
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "P" in the wordmark on all sides of the logo. Never crowd the logo with other elements.
Minimum sizes: Full lockup — 120px wide (digital), 1.5 inches (print). Mark only — 32px (digital), 0.4 inches (print).

Logo don'ts
Never rotate, distort, or add effects to the logo. Never change the logo colors outside approved variations. Never place the logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast. Never add outlines, shadows, or gradients to the logo. Never recreate or modify the hexagonal mark.

Wordmark update
The wordmark has been updated from "PRONOVA DESIGNS" to "PRONOVA" only. All materials must use the updated wordmark. Legacy "Pronova Designs" branding should be retired from active use.
SECTION 04
Color system


Our color system balances dark luxury foundations with electric accent colors. The obsidian base keeps things premium; the violet, cyan, and magenta accents bring the psychedelic futurism to life.

The Pronova gradient
Our signature gradient flows from Electric Violet (#7B2FFF) through Cyan Plasma (#00F0FF). This gradient appears on buttons, hero backgrounds, accent lines, and data visualizations. It's the most recognizable element of the visual system after the logo.
Use the gradient sparingly — it should feel special, not wallpaper. One gradient element per composition is the general rule.



Usage ratios
Dark backgrounds (obsidian + charcoal): 70% of total composition. White/lavender text: 20%. Accent colors: 10%. The accents should feel like strategic punctuation, not primary coverage.

Print considerations
For print materials, convert all hex values to CMYK. The obsidian and charcoal backgrounds translate well to rich black (C:60 M:40 Y:40 K:100). Accent colors should be specified as spot colors (Pantone) for critical brand materials.
SECTION 05
Typography


Our type system uses geometric sans-serifs that read modern and premium. No serifs — they fight the futuristic positioning.



Type scale
Hero / display: 48–72px. Page title: 32–40px. Section heading: 24–28px. Subheading: 18–20px. Body: 16px. Caption / small: 12–14px.

Typography rules
Always use sentence case for headers — only capitalize the first word and proper nouns. Maintain generous line height (1.5–1.7 for body, 1.1–1.3 for display). Never use more than two weights on a single page. Letter spacing on uppercase labels: 0.05–0.1em.
SECTION 06
Voice & tone


The Pronova voice is conversational, direct, and unapologetically real. We talk like smart people explaining things to other smart people — no corporate padding, no buzzword bingo.

Conversational principle
Write like you're explaining something to a smart friend over coffee. Be direct without being stiff. Be specific without being academic.


Em-dashes for asides
Use em-dashes liberally to insert thoughts, clarifications, or commentary mid-sentence. This creates a natural speaking rhythm that feels human.
Examples: "We build the entire pipeline — from first click to closed deal." ... "Your potential customers are searching right now — and if you're not on page one, they're finding your competitors instead."

Colorful vocabulary
Use specific, vivid words instead of generic ones. This is where personality comes through.


Authentic about challenges
Don't pretend everything is easy or perfect. Acknowledging difficulties builds credibility and distinguishes us from every other agency blowing smoke.

SECTION 09
Content guidelines by format


Website copy
Lead with the problem, not the solution. Use specific numbers and results, not vague claims. Keep paragraphs under four sentences. Every page needs a clear call to action. Lead with capability, not geography — "based in Texas" reads better than "Brownsville TX company" when pitching premium work.
Proposals
Match formality to the client — government RFPs need more structure than startup pitches. Front-load the value proposition. Be specific about deliverables, timelines, and pricing. Include process details so clients know how we work. Always use the current Pronova brand system, never legacy materials.
Sales copy
Lead with the problem. Use specific numbers from real client results. Include case studies when possible. Avoid hype words — let the results speak for themselves. The goal is to inform and persuade, not to pressure.
Blog posts
Personal narrative is fine — share experiences and lessons learned. Use subheadings to break up long content. Include specific details (names, places, numbers) rather than vague descriptions. End with actionable takeaways, not generic conclusions.
Social media
Keep the same voice, compressed. No hashtag spam — three max per post. Share work in progress and behind-the-scenes content. Respond to comments within two hours during business hours. Never use emojis in professional posts.
